Short Story: The Truth About Idaho

by John Smith

Many people think that Idaho is some place in the middle of nowhere. Honestly, a lot of them don’t really know the difference between Idaho or Iowa. They the two places interchangeably. Little do they know, Idaho is much different than Iowa. Idaho shares borders with Washington, Nevada, Utah, Montana, Wyoming, Oregon. Even a tiny portion borders Canada. I challenge you to name another state that has so many next door neighbors. Truth is, if you said Missouri and Tennesee, you’re right. They have eight bordering states. So Idaho doesn’t have the most bordering states. That doesn’t matter so much, but you know what does matter? What Idaho has to offer.

Idaho shines in comparison to other states. Literally. It is not nicknamed “The Gem State” for nothing! In Idaho, almost every type of known gemstone has been found, including the largest diamond discovered in the U.S. Not only does Idaho’s dirt contain gemstones, but one third of the potatoes grown in the United States.

Many people are surprised at the different scenery found in Idaho. From the trees and lakes of the Northern panhandle, to the southern farmlands, and the mountains, rivers, and valleys in between. So much of Idaho is an exciting place to explore. Many people are drawn to Idaho as a permanent residence. The beautiful scenery, relatively low cost of living, and the kind people who reside there make for a great place to call home.

Truthfully, Idaho is a little bit of Heaven on Earth.
