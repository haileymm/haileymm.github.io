Fun Day Trips for Families

by Bill Williams

When the weather is warm, there are many opportunities for families to get out and explore what Southeast Idaho has to offer. The pandemic has created a need for safe, fun activities for families, and there is not a better way to spend the day than to head outdoors with those you love. That is why we have compiled a list of fun family day trips that will get you outside and bring you together.

Craters of the Moon - If your family would like to see a volcano (inactive of course), climb around on lava rocks and explore lava tube caves, then this is the place for you. There are many different hikes available for all skill levels that take anywhere from an hour to a whole day.

Darby Wind Caves - For those families with teenagers, Darby wind caves may be a great option. The hike is 5 miles round trip and some of the attractions include wild flowers and waterfalls. This hike is not for beginners, so it is reccomended not to bring young children.

Mesa Falls - A popular spot for many, this easy walk will be a great place to visit in the heat of the summer. The cool mist from the falls will be welcome as you take in the beautiful scenery. It is a fee area but includes bathrooms. Your family will enjoy watching the rushing water and maybe even some kayakers at the bottom of the falls.

Idaho has some of the best scenery anyone could ask for. Get outside with your family this weekend and enjoy the place we call home!
